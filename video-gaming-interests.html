Videogaming will always be near and dear to my heart as it was my way of managing my time growing up. Gaming was a reward from my parents when I would finish homework, complete chores around the home, and tend to my father's animals. I grew up in a ranch and gaming was often seen by parents as a waste of time. At school, gaming was my way to to connect with my peers. My peers did not care to talk about tending to goats, cows, ostriches, dogs, and other farm animals, but they did care to talk about grinding levels in Runescape, appreciate of games like Half-Life, and custom game modes in Warcraft. I do admit, I grew up a nerd with a country twist. My parents never banned gaming luckily, but I do appreciate that gaming allowed me to meet new people offline and online. Luckily, quite a bit of games are being recreated such Half-Life into Black Mesa which are a nice bite of nostalgia
